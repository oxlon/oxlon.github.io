Everything on this page is a demonstrator built on synthetic data. These are pro/-/-concept demonstrators built on synthetic data; they illustrate that the tax-risk and data-mining methods are sound but would require the Ministry's transaction-level VAT micro-data to be operationalised on genuine taxpayers. It shows the methods are sound and would run unchanged on genuine transaction-level micro-data once the Ministry supplies it; the figures are illustrative, not findings about real taxpayers.

Tax-risk and anomaly detection

An isolation forest screens a synthetic panel / 600 firms for the signature / carousel / missing-trader fraud — input VAT far exceeding plausible turnover, very high refund ratios, near-zero employment. Of 30 injected anomalies it catches 24 (recall 80 per cent) at 80 per cent precision — a realistic unsupervised result. On real VAT micro-data the same pipeline would rank live taxpayers by risk for audit.

Economic clustering (data mining)

A k-means engine groups economic units by structure into 3 clusters — oil-linked, non-oil tradable, domestic services — the kind / unsupervised segmentation the data-mining specification calls for, here on synthetic sector data and extensible to regions and firms.
