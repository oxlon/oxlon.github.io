Why longer and higher-frequency data matters

The annual sample / about twenty-five usable observations is the binding constraint on the precision / every multivariate estimate. It widens the impulse-response bands, limits the number / regressors that can be identified, leaves the exchange-rate pass-through bounded rather than point-identified, and prevents estimation / the quarterly structural model. Quarterly national accounts would roughly quadruple the effective sample and permit the five-variable model / the technical design; reliable, revision-managed series with consistent definitions across the statistical transitions / the 1990s and 2000s would further improve the estimates, and several early observations were excluded for this reason.

Model limitations

  • Small-sample imprecision. The vector autoregression and its variance decomposition rest on twenty-five annual observations; the responses are directionally reliable but the bands are wide, and the decomposition shares should be read as orders / magnitude.
  • Pass-through is bounded, not point-identified. Simultaneity between the exchange rate and inflation means the causal pass-through is estimated as a range (about 0.10‚Äď0.21); resolving it to a point requires an instrument, which the quarterly data would support.
  • The simulator is first-order. Its channels are proportional and contemporaneous: it omits non-linearities, dynamic feedback and regime changes, and the debt path simply accumulates the implied deficits. It is built for rapid, auditable what-if analysis; the dynamic and structural questions are the province / the vector autoregression and the DSGE.
  • Fan-chart bands are empirical. The probability intervals are derived from the historical dispersion / forecast errors, not from a structural predictive density; they are deliberately wide by 2030, reflecting the economy's dependence on the oil price.
  • The external benchmark is an independent comparator. The IMF World Economic Outlook is shown alongside the Oxlon forecast as an independent cross-check, constructed at a different date and on different assumptions; across the forecast horizon the two are nonetheless closely aligned (mean gaps / about 0.1 point on growth and 0.6 on inflation over 2026‚Äď29), which is documented on the Forecast Benchmark page.
  • Structural models are calibrated. The DSGE is calibrated at conventional values rather than estimated, and the input-output coefficients are illustrative pending the /ficial supply-use table.
  • The oil-fund transfer is discretionary. The transfer from the State Oil Fund is set by decision, not formula; its representation as a function / the non-oil deficit is an approximation, disclosed in the fiscal outputs.
  • The oil price is not forecast. No model forecasts it reliably at budget-relevant horizons; the scenario approach is the appropriate response, and all three paths are reported together.
