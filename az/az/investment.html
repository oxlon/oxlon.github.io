Investment in ratios: where Azerbaijan stands

Read as a share / output, Azerbaijan's investment effort has fallen markedly: gross fixed capital formation was about 28 per cent / GDP in 2015 but only about 17 per cent by 2024, as the post-construction oil economy matured and large pipeline projects completed. Net foreign direct investment has turned small, around 0.3 per cent / GDP, so capital formation is now overwhelmingly domestically financed. These World Bank ratios frame the level forecast that follows.

Figure 9.1. Investment in international-comparable ratios: gross fixed capital formation and net foreign direct investment, per cent / GDP, 2000–2024. Source: World Bank World Development Indicators.

The accelerator and the oil cycle

Gross fixed capital formation is modelled as an accelerator-plus-oil process: the growth / real investment responds to the pace / real output growth, the accelerator mechanism under which firms add to the capital stock when demand is expanding, and to the oil cycle, with a lag capturing the gradual adjustment / investment plans:

$$ \Delta\log(I_t)=\alpha+\beta\,g_t+\delta\,\Delta\log(\mathrm{oil}_t)+\rho\,\Delta\log(I_{t-1})+\varepsilon_t $$(I1)

where Δlog(It) is the growth / real fixed investment, gt real GDP growth, Δlog(oilt) the change in the log oil price and Δlog(It−1) lagged investment growth; α is the constant, β the accelerator (+0.27), δ the oil-price sensitivity (+0.065), ρ the persistence (+0.15) and εt the disturbance. Estimated by ordinary least squares on 2013–2025 actuals, R² = 0.14.

Both channels carry the expected positive sign, the accelerator at +0.27 per point / output growth and the oil-price sensitivity at +0.065, so a ten per cent rise in the oil price adds roughly 0.7 per cent to investment growth. Rolling the equation forward along the Oxlon growth and oil paths gives the level forecast in Figure 9.2.

Figure 9.2. Total fixed investment: outturns to 2025 and the Oxlon accelerator projection for 2026–2030 with 50% and 80% probability bands (AZN million).

Total fixed investment reaches about 25 billion manat by 2030 on the Oxlon path, close to and a little below the Ministry's own projection / 27 billion. The investment-to-GDP ratio eases from about 17.3 per cent in 2025 to roughly 16 per cent by 2030 (Figure 9.3), as nominal output outpaces capital formation across the horizon.

Tənqidi qiymətləndirmə
The relationship is estimated on only about ten usable annual observations, and on so short a sample none / the coefficients is statistically significant: the accelerator carries a p-value / 0.83, the oil-price term 0.57, the lagged-investment term 0.69 and the constant 0.62, while the fit is modest (R² = 0.14). The signs are therefore reliable but the magnitudes are no more than indicative. Two further caveats bound the projection. The domestic and foreign split is imposed as a fixed recent-average share rather than modelled, so it cannot shift with the cycle. And foreign investment is lumpy: it is driven by individual oil and gas projects that arrive in discrete steps, and is not well captured by the smooth estimated equation, which is better read as a description / the aggregate trend than / the foreign component.
