Qurulma v…ô transformasiyalar

Output is built from the production side: real value added is projected by sector and aggregated, and nominal value added follows from the sectoral deflators, with the headline identities enforced. The monthly consumer price series is a month-on-month index (100 denotes no change); it is cumulated to a price level from which the year-on-year inflation rate used in estimation is derived. Monetary aggregates and domestic rates are taken from CBAR; foreign policy rates come from FRED/ECB; Brent enters as a scenario parameter; and sovereign risk is constructed from bond yields rather than assumed.

$$ spread_t = YTM^{AZE}_t - YTM^{UST, matched}_t $$(D1)

Sovereign risk premium. The primary series is built from Refinitiv/LSEG Azerbaijan sovereign Eurobond yield-to-maturity data for XS1678623064 and XS1678623734, matched to US Treasury constant-maturity benchmarks. WRDS/CRSP data are used for benchmark validation and robustness.

$$ P^{m}_t=\Big(\sum_i w_i\,P^{world}_{i,t}\Big)\,e_t,\qquad \pi^m_t=\Delta\log P^{m}_t $$(D2)

Import-price index. The model-ready import-price input is a trade-weighted import deflator on Azerbaijan's import structure ‚ÄĒ manufactures (machinery, vehicles, chemicals, metals; w ‚Čą 0.68), food (‚Čą 0.18) and energy (‚Čą 0.14) world-price indices Pworld, expressed in manat e under the de-facto peg. This is the standard import-deflator construction, grounded in the World Bank / IMF commodity series and the SSC import structure.

$$ \pi^{core}_t=\pi^{\,\text{non-food}+\text{services}}_t $$(D3)

Core / base inflation. The model-ready core input is the ex-food core taken directly from the SSC consumer-price breakdown ‚ÄĒ the ‚Äúnon-food products & services‚ÄĚ component (2.9 per cent in 2024) ‚ÄĒ reported beside CBAR's official base-inflation readings (4.4 per cent in 2024) as the validation anchor. The two differ by construction (CBAR's base excludes administered and seasonal items); both are carried in the system.
