Tax-risk and anomaly detection

An isolation forest screens a synthetic panel of 600 firms for the signature of carousel / missing-trader fraud — input VAT far exceeding plausible turnover, very high refund ratios, near-zero employment. Of 30 injected anomalies it catches 24 (recall 80 per cent) at 80 per cent precision — a realistic unsupervised result. On real VAT micro-data the same pipeline would rank live taxpayers by risk for audit.

Economic clustering (data mining)

A k-means engine groups economic units by structure into 3 clusters — oil-linked, non-oil tradable, domestic services — the kind of unsupervised segmentation the data-mining specification calls for, here on synthetic sector data and extensible to regions and firms.
