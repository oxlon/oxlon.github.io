Impulse Responses

The dynamic response of the economy to an oil-price shock, estimated with a structural vector autoregression. This section sets out the model, the identification scheme, and the interpretation of each response.

Model and identification

Let $z_t=(\,\Delta\log p^{oil}_t,\ \Delta\log e_t,\ \pi_t\,)'$ denote oil-price growth, the change in the exchange rate and inflation. The reduced-form vector autoregression of order one is

$$ z_t = c + A\,z_{t-1} + u_t,\qquad \mathbb{E}[u_t u_t']=\Sigma $$(V1)

Structural shocks $\varepsilon_t$ are recovered through a recursive (Cholesky) decomposition $\Sigma=PP'$, with $u_t=P\varepsilon_t$, under the ordering oil price, then exchange rate, then inflation:

$$ \varepsilon_t = P^{-1}u_t,\qquad P\ \text{lower-triangular} $$(V2)

The ordering reflects defensible timing assumptions: the world oil price is not contemporaneously influenced by domestic variables; the managed exchange rate adjusts next; and consumer prices respond last. The impulse response at horizon $h$ is the $h$-period-ahead effect of a one-standard-deviation structural shock, $\partial z_{t+h}/\partial \varepsilon_{j,t}$. The model is estimated on annual data (2000–2024, 25 observations); the bands are two standard errors.

Interpretation

The response of inflation is positive on impact, at approximately 2.2 percentage points, and dissipates within about three years. This pattern is characteristic of an economy that imports a large share of its consumption and exports oil: higher oil prices ease the constraint on the managed parity and feed through to consumer prices, after which the effect decays. The exchange-rate response is consistent with the same mechanism. The identification follows the oil-shock literature (Kilian, 2009).

High-frequency evidence

A monetary vector autoregression was also estimated on the monthly panel (2005-02–2024-12, 239 observations). At monthly frequency the direct pass-through from the oil price to consumer prices is small, and the policy-rate response exhibits the price puzzle commonly found in such systems. Both features indicate the absence of the exchange-rate and activity channels at this frequency and motivate the quarterly national-accounts structural model set out in the Limitations and Delivery sections.
