Technical Report

A complete account of the methods and results: estimation, diagnostics, interpretation, and the boundaries of each model's validity.

1 · Data and identities

The annual dataset is reconstructed from the Ministry's workbooks: the statutory tables under clause 2.4.1 (9,356 observations, 2013–2024 actual and 2025–2030 projected) and an annual inflation block from 1995, supplemented by a monthly panel of 360 observations. Two accounting identities were verified on the Ministry's data — gross domestic product as the sum of oil and non-oil value added (maximum error 0.05 per cent) and the nominal reconstruction from real growth and the deflator (0.00 per cent). The forward engine reproduces the official 2025–2030 path exactly. The annual core is therefore sound and ready for operation.

2 · The inflation equation

The pass-through equation explains 62% of the in-sample variance of annual inflation, with a positive, well-determined exchange-rate coefficient (+0.21 percentage points per one per cent depreciation), consistent with the literature on oil-exporting economies. The out-of-sample comparison (section 4) shows that the multivariate linear specification extrapolates poorly on the short sample; explanatory fit and forecast accuracy are distinct objectives, and the equation is best used for interpretation while forecasting draws on the model combination.

3 · Vector-autoregression impulse responses

A reduced-form vector autoregression over 2000–2024 (25 observations), identified by the recursive ordering oil price, exchange rate, inflation, yields an impact inflation response of approximately 2.2 percentage points to a one-standard-deviation oil shock, decaying within about three years. The ordering rests on standard timing assumptions. With twenty-five annual observations the responses are directionally reliable but imprecisely estimated; the bands are correspondingly wide. The quarterly structural model of five variables is the appropriate refinement and is described in the Limitations section.

4 · Model comparison

Every model was evaluated by expanding-window walk-forward testing. The most accurate annual model is Neural Net (MLP) (root-mean-square error 3.12, skill +0.38 over a random walk), with the univariate time-series model and tree-based ensembles close behind; the over-parameterised linear models record negative skill. On the monthly series, a long short-term memory network forecasts inflation twelve months ahead with a root-mean-square error of 4.547, against 6.678 for persistence, an improvement of 32%. The determinant ranking places the world food price, the exchange rate and the oil price foremost, corroborating the structural relationship.

5 · Structural models

The three-equation New-Keynesian model, solved by undetermined coefficients, returns correctly-signed responses (a monetary tightening lowers output and inflation; a cost-push shock generates the output-inflation trade-off). The input-output block yields output multipliers and propagates an oil final-demand shock through inter-industry linkages. These models serve as theory-consistent cross-checks; the supply-use coefficients are illustrative pending the official table.

6 · Scenarios

The oil price enters as a parameter rather than a forecast. The three statutory paths (baseline USD 65, conservative −15 per cent, stress −30 per cent) map, through an oil-sector elasticity of 0.90 and the oil share, to output deviations of approximately 0, −3.9 and −7.8 per cent in 2025. These are first-order responses; the dynamic second-round propagation is the function of the structural vector autoregression.

7 · Estimation conventions

Error-correction specifications require unit-root and cointegration testing; the medium-term core is calibrated and then validated; the structural vector autoregression is identified recursively. Acceptance requires reproduction of the official forecast and a non-oil growth root-mean-square error below 1.5 percentage points before any extension. Re-estimation is annual, timed ahead of the September submission, with a monthly refresh during execution.
