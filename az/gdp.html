Output and Growth

The output projection combines a sectoral, production-side build-up with the national-accounts identities. This section reports the baseline path and its uncertainty, the composition of growth, and the position of the economy in the cycle.

The baseline and its uncertainty

Real output growth is projected by aggregating sectoral value added, with the identities of the Modelling Framework enforced. The fan in Figure 7.1 sets the central path against 50 and 80 per cent probability bands and against the IMF World Economic Outlook. The Ministry's baseline is more optimistic than the IMF's for the near term — 2026 growth of 2.5 per cent against the IMF's lower path — a difference that matters for revenue planning and is examined in the Scenario Analysis.

Figure 7.1. Real GDP growth: outturns to 2024, the 2025 IMF estimate, and the Ministry baseline for 2026–2030 with 50% and 80% probability bands; the IMF World Economic Outlook is the external comparator.

The composition of growth

Growth is decomposed into the contributions of the oil-and-gas and non-oil sectors, each weighted by its share in output. The non-oil economy is the sole engine of growth across the horizon; the oil sector subtracts from growth as mature fields decline, contributing -0.2 percentage points in 2030. This is the central structural fact of the outlook and the reason the non-oil base is the focus of policy.

The cyclical position

Potential output and the output gap are estimated with a Hodrick-Prescott filter and feed the Phillips curve and the policy rule. The gap is economically coherent — a trough of −7.4 per cent in 2020 and recovery to slightly above trend by 2024 — which provides a consistent basis for the inflation and revenue projections.

Estimation note
The output projection reproduces the Ministry's published path from its sectoral assumptions, which is the acceptance condition for the engine. The bands are derived from the historical dispersion of growth and are wide by 2030, reflecting the dependence of the economy on the oil price.
