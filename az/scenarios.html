Scenario Analysis

The medium-term outlook is conditioned on assumptions that may not hold. Scenario analysis quantifies the consequences of the principal risks — above all the oil price — for output, inflation and the public finances.

Why scenarios, not a single forecast

No model forecasts the oil price reliably at budget-relevant horizons; the futures curve and survey forecasts are, in practice, little better than a random walk (Kilian, 2009). The disciplined response is not to predict the price but to condition on a small set of plausible paths and report the outcomes of each. Decree No. 75 institutionalises this in requiring three scenarios — baseline, pessimistic and optimistic — and the system reports them together throughout.

A comparison of named scenarios

The simulator's transmission channels are applied to a set of policy-relevant scenarios, reporting the 2030 outcome for each. The oil-driven scenarios dominate the fiscal result; a devaluation is principally an inflation event; fiscal consolidation improves the balance at a modest cost to output.
