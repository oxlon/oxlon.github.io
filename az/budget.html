The 2025 outturn
The 2025 state budget closed with revenue of AZN 39.1 billion, expenditure of AZN 38.6 billion and a surplus of AZN 527.6 million (State Statistical Committee, January 2026). The engine below models the consolidated structure — revenue by statutory head, including the State Oil Fund transfer — so its headline aggregate is wider than the narrower state-budget total; head shares are calibrated to the published consolidated structure pending the Ministry's audited head-level series.

Revenue by head (M2)

Each revenue head is projected on its own base: the State Oil Fund transfer on the oil price, value-added and excise taxes on consumption, the profit tax on non-oil output, the income tax and social contributions on the wage bill, customs on imports. The oil fund alone supplies about 42 per cent of revenue — the single fact that dominates the budget.

The fiscal rule and the non-oil deficit (M4, M6)

Consolidated expenditure is checked each year against the Article 11-1 ceiling — non-oil revenue plus a sustainable, GDP-linked use of oil revenue. It remains within the ceiling throughout the baseline. The statutory anchor, though, is the deficit excluding the oil-fund transfer (clause 3.4.2.17): at -11 per cent of GDP in 2026, narrowing to -7 by 2030, it measures exactly how far spending outruns non-oil revenue — the structural reliance the rule exists to narrow.

Sequestration monitor (M5)

Clause 6.3-1: a 5 per cent receipts shortfall triggers partial sequestration; 10 per cent a deeper cut. About 45 per cent of expenditure is discretionary (cuttable); interest, social transfers and wages are protected. In the baseline surplus a moderate shortfall is absorbed; sequestration binds only when receipts fall enough to exhaust the surplus — which the oil scenarios in the Fiscal section show happens below about USD 45 a barrel.

Data status
The module structure is operational; the head-level revenue split is calibrated to the published consolidated-budget shares. The audited net revenue by head (State Tax Service, State Customs Committee) and paragraph-level execution (Ministry of Finance) are recorded in the data request, and replace the calibrated shares on transfer.
