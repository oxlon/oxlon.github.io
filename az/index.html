Medium-term macroeconomic forecasting under the Economic Management Information System

This report sets out the macroeconomic forecasting component of the Ministry's Economic Management Information System, developed under the work programme established by Cabinet of Ministers Decree No. 75. It documents the assembled data, the estimated models, the forecast across the prescribed directions, and the scenario and risk analysis required for the preparation of the State Budget.

Abstract

The Ministry prepares, by statute, a three-scenario medium-term macroeconomic forecast that constitutes the basis of the State Budget. This work consolidates the existing sectoral models and the deployed Financial Programming framework into a single, reproducible system; re-estimates and validates the behavioural relationships against the Ministry's data; and extends the apparatus with the econometric and machine-learning models specified in the project terms of reference. The resulting system reproduces the official 2025–2030 projection and provides the scenario, risk and decision-support capabilities the budget process requires. The analysis is conducted at annual frequency for the statutory projection and at monthly frequency for the high-frequency components; all results are reproducible from the accompanying code and the Ministry's source data.

Scope of the engagement

The terms of reference, set out in the work programme under Decree No. 75, specify ten forecast directions — output, inflation, the fiscal accounts, the external sector, the labour market, oil and gas, investment, the exchange rate, demography and the regions — together with a defined set of econometric and machine-learning models, four cross-cutting capabilities (scenario simulation, risk intelligence, executive reporting and data harmonisation), and an associated work programme. The mandate and the requested scope are reproduced in full in the Mandate and Legal Basis section, and the correspondence between the requested models and the delivered system is set out in the Modelling Framework.

Principal results

  • Growth and structural change. The baseline projects average real output growth of 2.5 per cent over 2025–2030. The non-oil economy continues to gain share, from 68 to 81 per cent of output by 2030, as oil and gas production matures; the oil sector contracts in real terms across the horizon.
  • Inflation. Consumer-price inflation is governed primarily by exchange-rate and import-price pass-through; a one per cent depreciation of the manat raises prices by approximately 0.21 percentage points. The baseline path rises from 1.7 per cent in 2025 to 7.2 per cent in 2030.
  • The principal risk. Oil-price movements dominate the macro-fiscal outlook: a thirty per cent decline in the oil price reduces nominal output by an estimated 7.8 per cent relative to the baseline and contracts oil-related revenue substantially. The price is therefore treated as a scenario parameter, reported across baseline, conservative and stress paths.
  • Forecast performance. Among the models evaluated out of sample, a parsimonious neural network and a univariate time-series model are most accurate on the annual series; on the monthly series a long short-term memory network reduces twelve-month-ahead inflation forecast error by approximately one third relative to a no-change benchmark.

The estimates summarised here are documented, with diagnostics, in the Regression Results, Impulse Responses, Machine-Learning and Structural Models sections; their limitations are stated in the Limitations and Data Requirements section.
