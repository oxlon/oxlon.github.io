Limitations and Data Requirements

The constraints on the present analysis and the data that would extend it. These are factual and bear directly on the accuracy and scope of the forecast.

Data limitations

The principal constraint is the length and frequency of the available series.

Why longer and higher-frequency data matters

The annual sample of approximately twenty-five usable observations is the binding constraint on the precision of every multivariate estimate. It widens the impulse-response bands, limits the number of regressors that can be identified, and prevents the estimation of the quarterly structural model. Quarterly national accounts would approximately quadruple the effective sample and permit the five-variable structural vector autoregression specified in the technical design. Reliable, revision-managed series — with consistent definitions across the statistical transitions of the 1990s and 2000s — would further improve the estimates; several early observations were excluded for this reason.

Model limitations

  • Monthly transmission. The monthly monetary vector autoregression exhibits the price puzzle and a muted oil pass-through, reflecting the absence of the exchange-rate and activity channels at that frequency. It is reported as supporting evidence rather than as a headline result.
  • The oil-fund transfer. The transfer from the State Oil Fund is set by decision rather than formula. Its representation as a function of the non-oil deficit is an approximation; where the transfer is changed for reasons unrelated to fiscal need, the forecast will err, and this is disclosed in the relevant outputs.
  • Computable general equilibrium. The supply-use coefficients are illustrative pending the official table; the structure is production-ready but the magnitudes are not yet calibrated to Azerbaijan.
  • The oil price. No model forecasts the oil price reliably over budget-relevant horizons. The scenario approach is the appropriate response, and all three paths are reported together.
  • Sample-dependent pass-through. The pass-through coefficient is estimated on the annual sample and will be refined as the monthly panel is incorporated into the estimation.
