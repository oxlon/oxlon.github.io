Risk Intelligence

A macro-fiscal risk heat-map, an early-warning panel and a KPI scorecard — all computed from the system's own forecasts and scenarios, not asserted. The picture is a comfortable baseline sitting on a high underlying vulnerability to one factor: the oil price.

Risk heat-map

Each risk is scored on likelihood and impact (1–5); the product places it on the grid. A sustained oil-price slump and the structural non-oil fiscal gap sit top-right — high likelihood and high impact — while debt and the exchange-rate peg are lower-likelihood but high-impact tail risks.

Figure 19.1. Risk heat-map: likelihood (horizontal) against impact (vertical); bubble size and colour show the combined score. Top-right is most serious.

Early-warning indicators

Each indicator compares a current or forecast value against a stated threshold and returns a traffic-light status. On the baseline most are green; the one amber flag is the non-oil deficit — the structural reliance on oil.

The reading
Things are comfortable now — most indicators green, debt low, the budget in surplus — but the underlying risk is high because the downside is severe and concentrated: nearly every serious risk traces to the oil price, transmitted through the structural non-oil deficit. The fiscal rule and diversification are the mitigants.
