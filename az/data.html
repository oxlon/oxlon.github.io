Series held

The headline macroeconomic series now span 1991–2025 — real GDP growth, the GDP deflator, CPI inflation, population and the oil price, sourced from the World Bank, IMF and FRED and spliced with the Ministry's data — with the sectoral detail (the oil/non-oil decomposition and deflators under clause 2.4.1) available from 2013; the canonical forecast extends to 2031. The statutory tables contribute 9,356 observations. The high-frequency dataset comprises six monthly series from 1995, used for the inflation and monetary analysis.

Construction

Output is built from the production side: real value added is projected by sector and aggregated, and nominal value added follows from the sectoral deflators, with the headline identities enforced (Modelling Framework). The monthly consumer price series is a month-on-month index (100 denotes no change); it is cumulated to a price level from which the year-on-year inflation rate used in estimation is derived. The monetary aggregates and interest rates are taken from the Central Bank; the Brent price is taken from FRED; the oil price enters the forecast as a scenario parameter.

Currency of the data

The official 2025 outturns, published by the State Statistical Committee in January 2026, are incorporated: 2025 is the last actual year and 2026 the first forecast year, and the verified 2025 figures replace the earlier estimates throughout. The forecast for 2026 onwards is the project's own skill-weighted ensemble, estimated through the 2025 actual. The table below sets that forecast against the IMF World Economic Outlook as an external check; across the forecast horizon the two are closely aligned, which is documented in full on the Forecast Benchmark page.
