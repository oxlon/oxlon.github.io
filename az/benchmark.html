Section 03 of 30

An independent, skill-weighted multi-model ensemble — nine models (AR, ARDL/ECM, production/HP potential, CAEM open-economy Phillips curve, target-anchored, Ridge, Random-Forest, Gradient-Boosting, and a Bayesian/shrinkage VAR), each back-tested by expanding-window one-step-ahead forecasting (2012–2024) — placed beside the three external references: the IMF 2026 Article IV (Country Report 26/112), the Ministry’s CAEM.xlsb model, and the prior site baseline. The ensemble reproduces the IMF/CAEM picture, validating the method; the prior baseline is the outlier and has been superseded by this canonical set across the site.

Does the IMF use the Ministry’s CAEM model?
No. The Article IV projections are sourced to “National authorities; and IMF staff estimates and projections,” and the report explicitly notes the authorities hold more optimistic growth views. The IMF and CAEM are the same methodological family (the IMF built CAEM through technical assistance) but are independent applications — which is precisely why they serve as mutual cross-checks here.
Method and integrity
Inverse-RMSE^2 weighted ensemble of 9 models, each back-tested by expanding-window 1-step-ahead (2012-2024). Models with no skill vs a random walk are dropped. GDP: pure extrapolation/ML show little skill, so the structural Potential-output and shrinkage models dominate; the small premium over IMF reflects current elevated Brent (~$90+ vs IMF ~$84). CPI: AR/Phillips/VAR carry skill. Bands from ensemble back-test RMSE.
