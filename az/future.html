Future Modules — 11.2, 11.3 & 11.4

Module 11 extends beyond the macroeconomic core (11.1) to microeconomic & sectoral forecasting (11.2), risk & scenario analysis (11.3) and economic-policy modelling (11.4). The data foundation for all three is already collected, exploratory analysis is complete, and the modelling architecture is in place. This page sets out what is ready today and what each module delivers in the next build phase.

11.2 — Microeconomic & sectoral forecasting

Firm-level and sector-level forecasting on top of the macro core: value added, output, employment and the business environment, by industry (NACE) and region. The Ministry's sectoral equations (MOE SNA / INDUSTRY / SOCIAL) are already reproduced and run, and the statutory sheets 2.4.1.2 / .3 carry sector value added today.

11.2 — what is in hand and what it delivers.
AspectStatus today
Data in handWorld Bank Enterprise Survey firm-level microdata (Azerbaijan 2024); SSC sector value added, output and employment by NACE; business demography (firm entry / exit); regional / rayon statistics; CRRC household survey.
Exploratory analysisSector value-added shares and growth, employment by sector, business birth/death dynamics — already feeding the 2.4.1.2 / .3 statutory sheets.
Modelling doneThe Ministry's sectoral equations reproduced; CGE and input–output models scaffolded; sector wage and employment equations validated in-sample (0.9–3%).
Delivers nextSector-by-sector forecasts on each industry's own equation, a firm-environment / competitiveness layer, and regional disaggregation.

11.3 — Risk analysis & scenario-based forecasting

Conditional forecasting across a very large scenario space — the contract's 4,095 (= 2¹²) and one-billion-plus (≈ 2³⁰) scenarios are combinatorial draws over the shock set plus Monte-Carlo sampling of the engine's covariance structure. The live solver already runs the three Decree-75 scenarios with nine shocks; the risk data is collected.

11.3 — what is in hand and what it delivers.
AspectStatus today
Data in handCBOE volatility (VIX) and oil-volatility (OVX) indices; Geopolitical Risk (Russia / Turkey / Ukraine); Economic Policy Uncertainty (partner economies); World Bank commodity-price paths; and a ready-made country-calibrated stress scenario — the IMF 2024 FSAP adverse case (−50% oil & gas, −30% manat).
Preliminary workThe interactive solver runs the baseline / pessimistic / optimistic triple over nine shock dimensions, with fan bands; the engine's residual covariance supports the scenario fan.
Modelling doneThe semi-structural engine, the shock taxonomy, and the volatility / correlation inputs needed for the scenario draws.
Delivers nextScenario-based conditional forecasting, the billion-path response engine, and risk fan charts / early-warning indicators.

11.4 — Economic-policy modelling & impact analysis

The response of the economy to policy decisions — Central Bank rate moves and demand-creating budget decisions — and the deeper transmission into the sectoral economy. The solver already responds to monetary (policy-rate) and fiscal (impulse) shocks; the policy-event data is collected.

11.4 — what is in hand and what it delivers.
AspectStatus today
Data in handCBAR policy-rate decision history (BIS Central-Bank-Policy-Rates / IMF IFS); the fiscal impulse from the MoF budget series; the policy instruments and transmission variables.
Preliminary workThe solver's Taylor-rule and fiscal blocks already translate a Central-Bank or budget decision into the 2026–2030 path; sectoral transmission runs through the input–output / CGE structure.
Modelling doneMonetary (Taylor rule, UIP / risk premium) and fiscal blocks; the identification of policy shocks from the dated decision series.
Delivers nextPolicy-response analysis for Central-Bank and budget decisions, deep sectoral impact, and equilibrium-model policy evaluation.
