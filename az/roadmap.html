Delivery and Next Steps

The complete model suite is implemented and evaluated. The outstanding work is the operationalisation of the system and the small number of components that require data held by the Ministry.

Status of each component

Delivered components are demonstrated in this document. Components marked "requires data" are specified and await the corresponding input set out in the Limitations section.

Operationalisation

Conversion of the implemented models into the production system operated by the Ministry. The workstreams proceed in parallel.
WorkstreamPeriodDetail
Automated data pipelineMonths 1–2Ingestion, schema validation and revision management
Quarterly series and structural modelMonths 1–3Acquisition or temporal disaggregation; identification
Budget-execution moduleMonths 2–4Six-module legal engine and sequestration, on receipt of the data
Production dashboard and statutory formatsMonths 3–5Executive dashboard; clause 3.7-1 submission templates
Knowledge transfer and governanceMonths 4–6Staff training; peer review; documentation

Delivered

The statutory engine, the equation catalogue, the regressions, the impulse responses, the machine-learning comparison, the deep-learning model, the DSGE and input-output models, the scenarios and the simulator.

Requires data

The quarterly structural model, the budget-execution module, and the transaction-level revenue analytics — each awaiting a specified input from the Ministry.

Resourcing

Approximately 46 to 61 person-months to reach the production system, with a peak team of six to eight, including data engineering, fiscal and legal expertise, and dashboard development.

Required decisions
Provision of the quarterly national accounts, the revenue and expenditure detail, and the implementing regulation of the fiscal rule; and confirmation of the system's status as an independent forecast validated against the official projection. These complete the outstanding components.
