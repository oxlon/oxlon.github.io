Scenario Simulator

An interactive macro-fiscal simulator. Seven policy and external levers are propagated, through documented transmission channels, to the 2025–2030 paths of output, inflation, the fiscal balance, public debt and the current account. The calculation runs in the browser from the estimated coefficients and the baseline projection; move a control, or load a preset shock, to see the effect.

How the simulator works

The engine is a transparent, first-order representation of the principal channels in the Azerbaijani economy. Each lever maps to a coefficient that is either estimated in this report or calibrated from the national accounts; the output paths are deviations from the Ministry baseline, with public debt accumulating the implied deficits. The design favours transparency over sophistication: every number can be traced to the coefficient below, which is the property a decision-support tool used in budget preparation requires.

Transmission channels and their coefficients. Output and inflation effects are first-order; the dynamic, multi-year propagation is the province of the structural vector autoregression and the DSGE.

Interpretation and caveats
The presets illustrate the principal risks: an oil price of USD 50 erodes the fiscal surplus and weakens output; a 2015-style devaluation raises inflation sharply through the pass-through; a global recession compounds the oil and external channels. The effects are proportional and do not capture non-linearities, regime changes or the full dynamic feedback; for those, the structural models in this section are the appropriate instruments. The simulator is intended for rapid, auditable what-if analysis, not point prediction.
