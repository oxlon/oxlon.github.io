Econometric Models

Notation
In the estimated equations below, DLOG denotes the log-difference (a growth rate), a lag is written (−1), and a term such as (T>2017) is a regime indicator active from that year. The reported coefficients are the deployed estimates; the literature is cited in the Literature section.

National accounts

The real expenditure components combine short-run dynamics with convergence to a long-run equilibrium, in the error-correction form (Engle and Granger, 1987; Pesaran, Shin and Smith, 2001):

$$ \Delta\log C_t=\alpha\,\Delta\log Y^d_t-\gamma\,\hat r^{L}_t-\lambda\,(\log C_{t-1}-\beta\log Y^d_{t-1})+\varepsilon_t $$(M1)

Consumption responds to real labour income and a correction towards its long-run relationship; investment responds to the oil-price index, domestic demand and real money, reflecting the dependence of capital formation on oil revenue.

Inflation and monetary

Consumer-price inflation is an open-economy Phillips curve combining inertia, imported inflation and the output gap (Galí and Gertler, 1999):

$$ \pi_t=\phi\,\pi_{t-1}+(1-\phi)\,\pi^{e}_t+\kappa\,\hat y_t+\theta\big(\Delta s_t+\Delta p^{*}_t\big)+\varepsilon_t $$(M2)

Labour and social

Wages follow a Phillips relationship in productivity and prices; Okun's law (1962) links unemployment to the non-oil output gap:

$$ \Delta W_t=\delta+\zeta\,\Delta(\text{productivity})_t-\eta\,\pi_{t-1}+\varepsilon_t $$(M3)

Sectoral wage equations of this form are estimated for each activity; the deployed model contains thirty labour and social relationships.

Oil revenue and the sovereign fund

$$ T^{SOFAZ}_t=q^{oil}_t\big(p^{oil}_t e_t-c\big)\,\sigma^{gov}\,\kappa_t,\qquad \kappa_t=f(\text{non-oil deficit gap}_{t-1}) $$(M4)

The transfer from the State Oil Fund is the single largest revenue line. Production is exogenous, following production-sharing-agreement profiles; the transfer ratio responds to the prior-year non-oil deficit. The dependence on this discretionary parameter is discussed in the Limitations section.

The catalogue

Every estimated equation embedded in the sectoral workbooks, with its coefficients, was extracted into a single machine-readable catalogue, which forms the calibration basis for the behavioural block.
