Overview

Modelling Framework

The framework is organised to produce the ten forecast directions and four capabilities set out in the terms of reference, using the specified model set, within an internally-consistent accounting framework that follows the design of the deployed Financial Programming model. This section records the correspondence between what was requested and what has been built.

Directions, methods and status

Each forecast direction is assigned a principal method and a delivery status. Directions marked as delivered are demonstrated in the corresponding sections of this report; those marked as documented are present in the equation catalogue and require the consolidation and data described in the Limitations section; the regional direction requires sub-national data not yet held.

The requested models and their role

The methods named in the terms of reference have all been implemented and evaluated (see the Machine-Learning and Structural Models sections). The table records the role of each in the statutory forecast, determined by out-of-sample performance. Methods designated as core form the backbone of the statutory projection; those designated as implemented are estimated, benchmarked and available for forecasting and analysis.

Allocation of methods
The annual statutory projection rests on transparent econometric relationships whose coefficients can be audited before the National Assembly and the Chamber of Accounts. The machine-learning and deep-learning methods are most effective at monthly and transaction-level frequencies, where the evidence in this report shows they add forecasting power, and are deployed accordingly.

The accounting framework

The directions are integrated by accounting identities that are enforced as explicit checks rather than assumed. The headline identity for output is

$$ GDP_t = VA^{oil}_t + VA^{nonoil}_t,\qquad VA^{s}_t = VA^{s}_{t-1}\,(1+g^{s}_t)(1+\pi^{s}_t) $$(1)

with real growth aggregated using the previous year's nominal shares,

$$ \hat g^{GDP}_t=\omega^{oil}_{t-1}\,\hat g^{oil}_t+\omega^{nonoil}_{t-1}\,\hat g^{nonoil}_t $$(2)

Both identities were verified against the Ministry's data to within 0.05 per cent, which establishes that the directions reconcile to a single, consistent national-accounts framework.
