Section 01 · Vision

A modern
economics
department,
built from
first principles.

↳ Calibrated against LSE, UCL, Bocconi, Tilburg, Oxford, Warwick & Rice core sequences

Qarabağ Universiteti is being founded at a moment when economics itself is changing — the discipline has become unmistakably empirical, computational and policy-engaged.

This proposal builds the BSc Economics around four convictions: that data and code are now core literacies of the economist, not optional electives; that theory must be applied, including to Azerbaijan's own diversification and post-conflict reconstruction; that graduates need a portfolio — a thesis, a replication package, a reading log — not just a transcript; and that the programme should remain internationally legible, so a Karabakh BSc transfers cleanly to a master's at any peer institution.

Pillar 01
Theory with rigour
A full intermediate sequence and a three-course econometrics spine.
Pillar 02
Data as native language
Python, R and Stata from Year 1; ML and deep learning by Year 3.
Pillar 03
Rooted in the region
A flagship course on the economy of Azerbaijan; Caucasus cases throughout.
Pillar 04
A defended thesis
Two-semester capstone with a public defence and replication package.
Section 02 · Architecture

A four-phase arc, one progression.

Each year answers a different question, and every year stands on the one before it.
PHASE 01
Y1

Foundations

Students build the runway: C1-level English, academic literacy, computer fluency, statistics, and the historical context needed before formal economics begins.

  • English to C1
  • Statistics with Python / R / Stata
  • Global Economic History
  • History of Economic Thought
PHASE 02
Y2

Core Theory

The analytical toolkit locks in: principles, intermediate micro and macro, mathematical methods, research design, and first econometrics.

  • Principles + Intermediate Micro
  • Principles + Intermediate Macro
  • Econometrics I: Cross-section
  • Data Lab: Fundamentals + ML
PHASE 03
Y3

Quantitative & Applied

Students move into applied economics: time-series and panel econometrics, strategy, industrial organization, development, finance, and deep learning.

  • Econometrics II + III
  • Game Theory + Industrial Organization
  • Development + Financial Economics
  • Data Lab: Deep Learning
PHASE 04
Y4

Synthesis

The final year turns the degree into a portfolio: thesis, Azerbaijan-focused applied work, international trade, political economy, and a chosen elective track.

  • Defended Thesis
  • Economy of Azerbaijan
  • International Trade + Political Economy
  • Five electives from the Year 4 library

Section 05 · The Data Spine

One continuous thread of code.

Four staged courses — running from Year 1 to Year 3 — turn data and code into a second native language for every graduate.
Stage 01 · Y1S2

Introduction to Statistics

From spreadsheets to a first reproducible Python/R notebook — the ladder onto the data spine.

Python · R · Stata
Descriptive stats
Hypothesis testing
Reproducibility
Stage 02 · Y2S1

Data Lab · Fundamentals

Python as the working tool. pandas, visualisation, scraping, git. The notebook becomes a primary deliverable.

pandas / tidy data
matplotlib · ggplot
Web scraping
Git & reproducibility
Stage 03 · Y2S2

Data Lab · Machine Learning

Supervised & unsupervised learning framed as a natural extension of econometric thinking — with strict guards on the prediction-vs-causation line.

Trees · forests · boosting
Regularisation
Cross-validation
Interpretability (SHAP)
Stage 04 · Y3S1

Data Lab · Deep Learning

Neural networks for text, images and high-dimensional economic data — and an honest discussion of where they don't belong.

Feed-forward & CNN
NLP embeddings
PyTorch / JAX
Causal-ML caveats
Underpinning every course

Every empirical exercise from Year 2 onward is delivered as a versioned, reproducible notebook.

Tooling

Python is the lingua franca. R is used in statistics & econometrics. Stata is taught as a literacy.

Outcome

A graduate who can do real economic data work on the first day of a master's, a job or a policy role.
