Monetary policy ESG & climate Working paper

Green shields.
How environmental performance
moderates monetary-policy
transmission.

Kansoy, F. & Stasiulaitis, D.

Oxford working paper · Revised Mar 2026

Download PDF ↓ BibTeX ↗
Abstract

We study how firms’ environmental performance reshapes the equity response to US monetary-policy surprises. Using high-frequency identification around every FOMC announcement since 2005, we separate the target channel from the path channel and document a structural break around the Paris Agreement.

After 2015, the response of high-ESG firms to an expansionary path surprise is materially stronger than that of their low-ESG peers — even controlling for size, leverage and sector.

The result survives a battery of robustness checks: alternative ESG scoring, sectoral fixed effects, and reverse-causality tests. Implications are drawn for central-bank communication and for the design of green supporting factors in prudential policy.
