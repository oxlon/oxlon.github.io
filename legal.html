About this document.

Oxlon Ltd is a private limited company registered in England and Wales (registration number to follow). Our registered office is in Oxford; our operating bases are in Oxford and London. This page sets out, in ordinary language, how we handle information about you when you interact with the oxlon.uk website or its successor domains, what rules govern your use of the site, and what cookies we rely on.

If something here is not clear, or if you think we have got something wrong, please write to privacy@oxlon.uk. We would rather be told.

Privacy notice.

This notice describes how Oxlon collects, uses, and protects personal data. It applies to the oxlon.uk website, the scoping-call form, the memo subscription, and any email you send us at an oxlon.uk address. It does not cover data processed under a specific client engagement — that is governed by the contract and data-processing agreement executed with each client.

What we collect, when, and why

We keep the surface area as small as possible. Three categories only:

  • Enquiry data. When you submit the scoping-call form or email us, we hold your name, role, institution, email address, and the body of your message for as long as needed to run the engagement or, if no engagement follows, for two years as a reference record.
  • Subscriber data. When you sign up to the Oxlon Memo, we hold your email address (and, if you supply it, your institution) for as long as you remain subscribed. You can unsubscribe at any time from any issue.
  • Site analytics. We use a privacy-respecting analytics tool (Plausible) to measure aggregate page views. We do not hold IP addresses, and we do not run cross-site trackers.

We do not sell, trade, or rent personal data. We do not use personal data to train any machine-learning model, whether our own or anyone else’s. We do not share personal data with third parties except the essential processors listed below.

Processors we rely on

  • Plausible Analytics — aggregate analytics, hosted in the European Union.
  • Buttondown — newsletter delivery.
  • Google Workspace — corporate email, calendar, and document storage for operational communications.
  • Cloudflare — content delivery, edge caching, and DDoS protection.

We execute a data-processing agreement with each of these processors and review the arrangement annually. If a processor changes, this page is updated and — where the change is material — we notify active subscribers.

Your rights

Under the UK GDPR you have the right to access the personal data we hold about you, to correct it, to have it erased, to restrict how we process it, to object to processing, and to have it ported to another controller. You can exercise any of these rights by writing to privacy@oxlon.uk. We will respond within one month and, in almost all cases, within one week. If you are unhappy with our response, you can complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office.

Retention, security, and breach

Enquiry records are held for two years from the date of last contact and deleted thereafter, unless an active engagement is in place. Subscriber records are held for the duration of the subscription plus a short grace period. Analytics records are held in aggregated form and are not tied to individuals.

Data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest. Access is restricted to named staff and logged. In the event of a personal-data breach likely to result in risk to you, we will notify you without undue delay and, in parallel, notify the Information Commissioner’s Office as required.

Terms of use.

These terms govern your use of the oxlon.uk website and any publicly accessible materials linked from it. They do not govern any engagement with Oxlon Ltd — engagements are covered by their own contract.

Who the site is for

The site is directed at institutional readers — staff at central banks, ministries, regulators, sovereign and pension funds, asset managers, and the research institutions that serve them. Content may be read and cited by anyone, but the advisory services described are not available to consumers and are not offered as personal financial or legal advice.

Intellectual property & citation

Written materials on oxlon.uk — working papers, insights, the memo archive, case studies — are the copyright of Oxlon Ltd and their respective authors. You are welcome to quote brief passages with attribution and a link to the original, and to share full PDFs of our working papers by URL. You may not republish long-form content in full without written permission. Commercial reuse (training data, derivative products, paid summarisation services) is specifically not permitted.

If you are a researcher or journalist and want broader permission than the default above allows, write to research@oxlon.uk. We default to yes for serious work.

What we do not represent

Nothing on the site is investment, legal, or tax advice. Forecasts, scenarios, and nowcasts are analytical outputs — presented with their assumptions and uncertainty bands — and are not recommendations. Figures in research papers and case studies are correct to the best of our knowledge at the time of publication, and are revised publicly when we find errors.

Third-party links are provided for the reader’s convenience. Oxlon does not control, and is not responsible for, content hosted on third-party sites.
