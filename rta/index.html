1.  Motivation & scope

Official Azerbaijani statistics are published as monthly batches, with a lag. Between releases the Ministry has no quantified, current reading of activity, and incoming figures are not automatically screened for errors. This module closes both gaps: it maintains a monthly nowcast of non-oil GDP — the part of the economy where the business cycle lives — from the faster activity indicators, with calibrated uncertainty; and it runs an automated data-quality / early-warning layer over the data flow. "Real-time" is interpreted faithfully for this data environment as a release-triggered refresh (monthly for most series, daily for financial data) — not sub-second streaming, which the data does not support.

5.  Data foundation

Source: Statistik data dinamika 05.06.2026.xlsx (State Statistical Committee · Central Bank · Ministry of Finance · State Customs Committee). Eight sheets are harmonised into one tidy store. Monthly figures are cumulative year-to-date; the pipeline classifies each series as flow / stock / rate and de-cumulates flows only (a level mistakenly de-cumulated would be corrupted — verified by an independent round-trip test).

6.  Methodology & models

Nowcast. The cyclical signal is in the non-oil economy; oil GDP is near-deterministic in production × price, so headline GDP follows by identity. The target is the monthly-momentum (single-month flow, year-on-year): on the smooth year-to-date series a random walk is unbeatable, whereas the monthly signal is where indicators add value — a documented choice, not an accident. The primary model is a mixed-frequency dynamic factor model (a single common activity factor, AR idiosyncratic terms) that natively handles the ragged edge; the benchmark is a transparent AR(1)+common-factor bridge that nests the random walk. Uncertainty is the headline model's residual dispersion (80% band).
Refs: Antolín-Díaz, Drechsel & Petrella (2024, J. Econometrics 238:105634); Bańbura & Modugno (2014); Mariano & Murasawa (2003).
Anomaly detection (15.7.2.3). Three layers: (i) rule gates — a year-to-date cumulative cannot fall (monotonicity), and the GDP identity non-oil + oil = total; (ii) statistical process control — robust median/MAD z-scores on indicator growth; (iii) machine learning — an Isolation Forest over the standardised panel for joint anomalies. Alerts are severity-tiered and carry a probable cause and a recommended action; the monotonicity gate is scoped to real-economy sheets where cumulatives are genuinely monotone.
