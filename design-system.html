The grammar of Oxlon.

A reference document. Tokens, type, components, and the rules that govern when to use what. The visual system is already in the deck — this page formalises it, so every surface of oxlon.uk can be built from the same vocabulary.

01 · Positioning
i

Wordmark & voice.

Oxlon is the canonical spelling — uppercase O, lowercase x-l-o-n. The italic treatment on lon is reserved for the wordmark, oversized displays, and pull-quote emphasis. In running prose the word stays upright.

Oxlon.

Intelligence, distilled for decision-making.

i
Write short.
Clauses, not paragraphs. Sentences carry weight; adjectives do not.
ii
Italic emphasis.
Reserve italic for verbs and nouns that do the lifting. Never for whole sentences, except pull-quotes.
iii
No slogans.
No "revolutionary", no "cutting-edge", no exclamations. The restraint is the brand.
iv
Numbering in lowercase roman.
i, ii, iii, iv — rendered in Cormorant italic. A deliberate editorial tic, from the deck.
02 · Colour
ii

Ink on paper, and five quiet accents.

Four families: inks for type and dark surfaces, papers for warm light surfaces, and three accent pairs — sage, powder, clay — for calm panels and emphasis. Clay is the only warm. It carries CTAs and highlighted chart paths.

Rule 01 · Dominance
One dominant surface per page.
Every page uses exactly one of ink-deep, paper, or stone as the page colour. Variant surfaces within — sage, powder, clay — appear only as panels.
Rule 02 · Accent hygiene
Never mix clay with sage.
Sage is calm, clay is warm-urgent. Within a single panel or band, pick one. They can coexist on a page but not in the same frame.
Rule 03 · Emphasis ladder
Italic > slate > clay > bold.
Emphasis in order of preference: italic Cormorant, slate colour, clay colour, then — rarely — bold. Never bold and colour.

03 · Typography
iii

Three families. Nine sizes. One rule.

Cormorant Garamond for display, IBM Plex Sans for body, IBM Plex Mono for labels and data. No fourth family, ever. Scale: 11 / 13 / 15 / 18 / 22 / 28 / 36 / 52 / 72.

05 · Motion & grid
v

Restraint is the motion.

Three allowed behaviours: a chart path drawing in on scroll, a 12-pixel fade-up on sections, and a pulsing dot for live state. Nothing else. No parallax, no carousels, no video backgrounds.

Motion 01 · Path draw
Chart paths.
Stroke-dash offset animation, 1.4s ease-out, once per chart on enter-view.
Motion 02 · Fade up
Section entrance.
12px translate-up, fade to 1, 400ms ease-out. Triggers when 20% into view.
A defensible forecast.
Motion 03 · Pulse
Live indicator.
2s infinite pulse on clay-pale dots. Signals live data; never decorative.
Grid · 12 columns · 44px gutter
06 · Iconography & imagery
vi

Data is the imagery.

No stock photography. No illustrated icons. Abstract geometric glyphs — the sector marks — carry visual identity where an icon would normally sit. When non-chart imagery is needed, use archival line-drawings of the Oxford and London skylines, never photography of people.

Ministry
Do
  • Draw your own SVG charts. They are the primary visual.
  • Use abstract geometric glyphs at low opacity as tile backgrounds.
  • Commission archival-style line drawings for the About page hero.
  • Let whitespace carry the composition. Density is editorial, not cluttered.
Don't
  • Use stock photography of "diverse teams in glass meeting rooms".
  • Add emoji or pictographic icons next to headlines.
  • Decorate with gradients outside the two allowed ambient gradients.
  • Use a fourth font, or Inter, Roboto, or system-ui as display.
